Life

Early Life

Aaron grew up in a small town near Athens, Georgia, where community events were a regular part of life. The town held numerous annual and monthly gatherings that brought everyone together for concerts, Relay for Life events, football games, parades, and more. During these years, Aaron formed many lasting friendships, with several friends still close to him today. He was actively involved in Sunday School and weekly youth events, where he met his best friends, and he spent many seasons playing baseball. Outside of these activities, Aaron developed a love for anime that has stayed with him into adulthood.

Early Adult Years

During his early adult years, Aaron moved to Atlanta to attend Georgia State University. Where he studied Philosophy. While working at a golf club, he met many great people and received valuable advice, much of which he'll carry throughout his career. Surprisingly, he didn't pick up golf as a hobby, though he enjoys competitive matches of Golden Tee. His time as a bartender there also developed a valuable skillset he continues to use today.

Career

Aaron has built a strong foundation in his career and is now focused on honing his skills as a software developer, with aspirations to become a digital product manager. He began as an Assistant Project Manager for EV Charger installations, then transitioned to Service Manager, where he gained valuable experience in Network Operations, Maintenance, and Customer Service. With a passion for software development, design, and strategy, Aaron had the opportunity to work alongside experience designers and software developers on the company's newly established Product Team. To accelerate his path toward product management, he enrolled in Emory's Coding Bootcamp, equipping himself with the skills to fast-track his goals. Aarons Linkedin

Adult Life

Currently, Aaron devotes most of his time to his career. Outside of work and coding studies, he stays active through weightlifting and running. Running on the treadmill doubles as a chance to catch up on his favorite shows, like The Lincoln Lawyer. Occasionally, Aaron spends time with friends, usually nearby, playing card games or joining a poker night. He also enjoys early 2000s sitcoms as his go-to comfort shows. While he wishes he had more time for video games, he rarely finds the chance to enjoy them.
